# Boostaro Ingredients Information

This repository organizes Boostaro ingredient information in a documentation-style format. It focuses on ingredient transparency, nitric oxide support, circulation support, vascular wellness, and the limits of ingredient-level research.

Ingredient information can change. Always verify the current product label and the main ingredients page before making a decision.

## Quick Answer: What Are Boostaro Ingredients?

The Boostaro ingredients discussed on the main ingredients page include Vitamin C, Vitamin K as Menaquinone/K2, and a proprietary blend containing L-Citrulline, L-Lysine HCL, Pine Bark Extract standardized to 95% proanthocyanidins, Magnesium Citrate, and Nattokinase.

The main website lists Vitamin C at 500 mg, Vitamin K at 100 mcg, and a 725 mg proprietary blend. Individual amounts inside the proprietary blend are not listed separately on the referenced page.

## Nitric Oxide and Circulation Support

Nitric oxide is a signaling molecule involved in blood vessel relaxation and vascular function. L-Citrulline is commonly discussed because it can support the body's arginine and nitric oxide pathways.

This does not mean Boostaro should be described as a treatment for ED or any disease. It remains a dietary supplement positioned around general wellness support.

## Antioxidant and Vascular Wellness Support

Vitamin C and Pine Bark Extract are commonly discussed in antioxidant and vascular wellness contexts. Antioxidant activity may be relevant to general wellness, but it does not prove predictable finished-supplement outcomes.

Vitamin K/K2 is often discussed in vascular-health education. Adults using anticoagulant medication should discuss Vitamin K intake with a qualified healthcare professional.

## Mineral and Energy Metabolism Support

Magnesium Citrate is commonly discussed as a mineral form related to normal muscle, nerve, and energy-metabolism functions. Magnesium context should be interpreted as general nutrition education, not a disease-treatment claim.

## What Ingredient-Level Research Can and Cannot Prove

Ingredient-level research can explain why certain compounds are included in circulation, nitric oxide, vascular wellness, or energy-metabolism discussions.

It cannot prove that the finished Boostaro formula produces specific results for every user. Product-specific outcomes depend on the full formula, dose, individual health status, medication use, lifestyle, and consistency.
